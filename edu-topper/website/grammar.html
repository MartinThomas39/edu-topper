SENTENCE

A sentence is a group of words which makes complete meaning.
Eg: Sonu is a student.

KINDS OF SENTENCES

There are four kinds of sentences. They are Assertive Sentence, Imperative Sentence, Interrogative Sentence and Exclamatory Sentence.

1. Assertive (Declarative) Sentence.

A sentence which makes a statement is called an Assertive Sentence.(പ്രസ്താവന നടത്തുന്ന വാക്യം)
Eg: The sun rises in the east.

An Assertive Sentence must always end with a full stop. An Assertive Sentence can either be Affirmative (positive) or Negative.

Eg: I love my parents. (positive)
Eg: I don’t love my parents. (negative)

Sentences which state positive facts are called Affirmative or Positive sentences.

Sentences which express negative ideas are called Negative sentences.
Negative words give negative meaning to the sentences.

Negative Words are:barely, few, hardly, little, neither, never, no, nobody, none, not, nothing, no-where, rarely, scarcely and seldom.

2. Imperative Sentence.

A sentence which expresses a command or a request is called an Imperative Sentence.

Eg: 1. Get out
Eg: 2. Please come in.

Imperative Sentences must always end with a full stop. Their subjects will always be ‘You’. But they don’t use the subject. It is understood.

Eg: (You) Get out. (You) Please come in.

3. Interrogative Sentence.

A sentence which asks a question is called an Interrogative Sentence. (To interrogate = to question)( ചോദ്യം ചോദിക്കുന്ന വാക്യം)

Eg: Who are you?

An Interrogative Sentence must always end with a mark of interrogation (?).

4. Exclamatory Sentence.

A sentence which expresses a strong feeling is called an Exclamatory

Eg: What a wonderful idea!

An Exclamatory Sentence must always end witha _ mark of exclamation (!).

Some grammarians say that there is one more type of sentence. It is Optative Sentence. A sentence which expresses a prayer, wish or desire is called an Optative Sentence.

Eg: May you ail pass!
Eg: Long live the President!

An Optative sentence must always end with a mark of exclamation (!).

Basic sentence patterns

'sentence pattern ' is the way a sentence is put together,the order of the elements in the sentence; sentence construction.

Seven Basic Sentence Patterns In English,

1. SV (Subject + Verb)


Eg: John slept.
John (Subject) slept (Verb).

2. SVO (Subject + Verb + Object).


Eg: Mr.Mohan likes dosa.
Mr. Mohan (Subject) likes (Verb) dosa (Object)



3. SVC (Subject + Verb + Complement).

Complement is a part of a sentence which complete What is sald about the subject or the object.

Eg: Meera is tall.
Meera (Subject) is (Verb) tall (Complement).

4. SVOO (Subject + Verb + Indirect Object + Direct Object)



Eg: My brother gave me a present.
My brother (Subject) gave (Verb) me (Indirect Object). a present (Direct Object).

5. SVOC (Subject + Verb + Object + Complement).

Eg: My brother made me angry.
My brother (Subject) made (Verb) me (Object) angry (Complement).

6. SVA (Subject + Verb + Adverbial).

Eg: John reached the shooting location.
John (Subject) reached (Verb) the shooting location (Adverbial)

7. SVOA (Subject + Verb + Obyect + Adverbial)

Eg: John put the bag down.
John(Subject) put (Verb) the bag (Object) down (Adverbial) .
